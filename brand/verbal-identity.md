# Neckarstücke — Verbal Identity

> Version 1.0 · April 2026  
> Status: Foundation document — governs all brand copy

---

## 1. Brand Name Rationale

**Neckarstücke** works because it operates on two levels simultaneously.

**The literal layer**: *Stücke* means pieces or fragments — the name says, literally, "pieces of the Neckar." Not the whole river, not the whole region — just the specific, selected pieces worth preserving. This signals curation from the first syllable.

**The resonance layer**: *Stück* in German also means a work — a theatrical work, a musical composition, something crafted with intention. *Neckarstücke* therefore also reads as "works about the Neckar" — artistic works, not reproductions.

**What the name avoids**: It contains no generic tourism vocabulary (no *Panorama*, *Aussicht*, *Romantik*, *Idylle*). It doesn't describe the product (no *Drucke*, *Poster*, *Prints*). It doesn't explain itself — it trusts the audience to bring their own meaning to the word.

**Why it resonates with the target audience**: People who know the region don't need explanation. For them, "Neckar" carries weight — the smell of the river in autumn, the silhouette of Minneburg, the winding road into Dilsberg. The name activates that emotional memory without narrating it. For people who have moved away, it's an immediate signal: *this is for me, specifically.*

## 2. Tagline

### Primary tagline

> **Das Neckartal. Unvergänglich.**
> 
> *The Neckar Valley. Timeless.*

This tagline works across German and English contexts. It is declarative, not promotional. It doesn't ask anything of the reader — it simply states what Neckarstücke believes. "Unvergänglich" (timeless / imperishable) carries more weight than "schön" (beautiful) or "einzigartig" (unique) — it speaks to permanence, to memory, to the fact that these places outlast the people who pass through them.

### Secondary / situational tagline

> **Für die, die bleiben. Und die, die gegangen sind.**
> 
> *For those who stay. And those who left.*

Use sparingly — in brand statements, about pages, and moments of direct audience address. This line names the emotional truth of the customer without being sentimental about it.

## 3. Tone of Voice

The Neckarstücke voice is built on four attributes. Each one pushes against a temptation that would weaken the brand.

---

### 3.1 Spezifisch — Specific

**Description**: Name things precisely. Avoid generalities. Don't say "a beautiful valley" — say "the bend in the Neckar south of Hirschhorn." Specificity is the signal that distinguishes a brand that genuinely knows a place from one that has merely photographed it.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Die Ruine der Minneburg thront 280 Meter über dem Neckar." | "Eine beeindruckende Burgruine mit toller Aussicht." |
| "Hirschhorn — dort, wo der Fluss eine Kurve macht und die Zeit langsamer wird." | "Entdecken Sie die schönen Orte des Neckar-Odenwald-Kreises." |
| "Fine Art Druck auf 310g Hahnemühle Photo Rag." | "Premium-Qualität auf hochwertigem Papier." |

---

### 3.2 Ruhig und sicher — Calm and confident

**Description**: Never shout. Never use superlatives to compensate for lack of substance. The work is good — let it speak. A calm, assured tone signals quality more effectively than breathless enthusiasm. Neckarstücke doesn't need to convince anyone — it simply presents.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Jedes Motiv entsteht als Einzeldruck auf Bestellung." | "Bestellen Sie jetzt und erleben Sie unvergleichliche Qualität!" |
| "Wir drucken, wenn Sie bestellen." | "Nur solange der Vorrat reicht — sichern Sie sich Ihr Exemplar!" |
| "Die Sammlung beginnt mit vier Orten." | "Riesige Auswahl an wunderschönen Motiven für jeden Geschmack!" |

---

### 3.3 Vertraut ohne Kitsch — Familiar without sentimentality

**Description**: The brand speaks with the insider knowledge of someone who grew up there, but without reaching for easy nostalgia. Heimweh is real — but the brand doesn't exploit it. The tone is warm but not sweet. It acknowledges emotional connection without performing it.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Ein Ort, den man kennt, bevor man ihn erklärt." | "Atmen Sie die Sehnsucht nach Heimat in jedem Bild." |
| "Wer einmal auf dem Dilsberg stand, weiß, was wir meinen." | "Tauchen Sie ein in die romantische Welt der Neckarlandschaft." |
| "Das Neckartal ist kein Ziel. Es ist ein Zuhause." | "Entfliehen Sie dem Alltag in die malerische Idylle des Neckartals." |

---

### 3.4 Respektvoll — Reverent

**Description**: The places are the subject. The brand is the medium. Copy should treat the motifs and the region with the same seriousness the prints are made with. This means no irony at the expense of the place, no over-familiarity, and no reduction of complex landscapes to punchlines.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Hirschhorn hat eine eigene Schwere — das spürt man im Motiv." | "Hirschhorn ist auch ganz nett, wenn man mal dort ist." |
| "Dilsberg braucht keine Worte. Es braucht Zeit." | "Dilsberg — so schön, dass man es kaum glauben kann!" |
| "Die Minneburg ist kein Touristenziel. Sie ist ein Ort." | "Perfektes Urlaubsmitbringsel für Neckar-Fans!" |

## 4. Vocabulary Guide

### Preferred terms

| Term | Usage |
|------|-------|
| **Motiv** | What the print depicts. Never "Bild" in isolation (too vague) or "Poster" (too generic). |
| **Kunstdruck / Fine Art Druck** | The product itself. Signals museum-level quality. |
| **Sammlung** | The product line / collection. Not "Sortiment" (retail) or "Auswahl" (selection). |
| **Ort** | Refers to the depicted location. Not "Sehenswürdigkeit" (tourist sight) or "Destination". |
| **Zuhause** | The emotional connection to the region. Preferred over "Heimat" in most contexts (less loaded). |
| **Auflage** | Framing of edition/exclusivity. "Kleine Auflage" conveys scarcity with dignity. |
| **Fine Art Papier / Hahnemühle** | Specific paper references signal genuine craft knowledge. |
| **Druck auf Bestellung** | "Printed to order" — a value statement, not a logistical disclaimer. |

### Terms to avoid

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| **Poster** | Mass-market connotation. Undermines premium positioning. |
| **Souvenir / Mitbringsel** | Tourist-trap register. Exactly what Neckarstücke is not. |
| **Wanddeko / Wandschmuck** | Reduction to decorative function. Treats the prints as accessories. |
| **Romantisch / Idyllisch** | Generic tourism descriptors. Say nothing specific about the place. |
| **Einzigartig / Unvergesslich** | Overused superlatives. Earn the claim, don't state it. |
| **Sehenswürdigkeit** | Frames the locations as tourist checkboxes. |
| **Produkt** | In customer-facing copy. Use "Motiv", "Druck", or "Kunstdruck" instead. |
| **Bestellen Sie jetzt** | Urgency marketing. The tone is invitation, not pressure. |

### Framing principles

- **The region, not the tourism board.** Write about places the way a local writes in a letter — not as a press release.
- **Quality through specificity, not adjectives.** "310g Hahnemühle Photo Rag, archival inks" says more than "hochwertige Materialien."
- **"Ort" beats "Sehenswürdigkeit."** A place is lived in and remembered. A sight is visited and photographed.
- **Emotional truth, not emotional manipulation.** Acknowledge that these places matter — but don't manufacture the feeling.
- **The print is an object, not a print-out.** It has weight, texture, presence. Language should reflect that.

## 5. Example Copy Lines

These examples demonstrate the voice across different contexts. They are models, not templates.

---

**Brand statement:**
> Neckarstücke ist eine Sammlung von Orten, die man kennt — aber noch nie so gesehen hat.

---

**Brand intro (about page / homepage):**
> Wir machen Kunstdrucke für Menschen, die wissen, wie der Neckar im Herbst riecht. Für die, die dort geblieben sind, und die, die gegangen sind und manchmal noch davon träumen.

---

**Product description opener (Minneburg):**
> Die Minneburg braucht keine Erklärung. Sie thront — und wer einmal dort war, weiß, was das bedeutet. Dieser Druck versucht nicht, das nachzuerzählen. Er zeigt es.

---

**Collection intro:**
> Die erste Sammlung beginnt mit vier Orten. Nicht weil das alles ist — sondern weil jeder Ort seine Zeit verdient.

---

**Quality statement:**
> Jeder Druck entsteht auf Bestellung, auf Fine Art Papier, mit archivfesten Tinten. Kein Lager, kein Kompromiss.
